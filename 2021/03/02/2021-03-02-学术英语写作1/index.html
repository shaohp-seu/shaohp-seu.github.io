学术英语写作1

language register title Nominalization Personal pronouns Word choice sentence variety
学术英语写作
发布日期:   2021-03-02
  1. language register
    1. What is “register”？
      1. A style of speaking and writing
      2. Represents a level of language formality
      3. 不同情境下的口语和写作英语要加以区别
    2. Different registers in English-5 Registers
      1. Static (frozen)
      2. Formal (regulated)
      3. Consultative (professional/ academic)
      4. Casual (group)
      5. Personal (intimate)
    3. What are the linguistic features of academic register？
      1. Wording (terminology术语, hyponyms同形同音异义词, initials)
      2. Nominalization——名词化
      3. Third person pronouns (impersonalization)
      4. Passive voice
      5. Tense
      6. Structural complexity
  2. How to write a title?
    1. Good titles VS bad titles
      1. bad
        1. single
        2. trivial resource
        3. too big
        4. overused
      2. good(SCI)
        1. specific
        2. concise
        3. informative
    2. 3-step method
      1. Choosing a topic-interesting
      2. Narrowing down-specific
      3. Paraphrasing-noun phrase
  3. Nominalization
    1. What?
      1. the process of converting verbs or
        adjectives whining a sentence to nouns.
    2. Why?
      1. increase information density and facilitate efficiency and
        accuracy of communication
      2. express abstract concepts (e.g., revolution, possibility)
      3. hide the agent behind an action
      4. improve flow of writing , maintain connections between
        ideas
      5. make writing more “written” and professional
    3. How?
      1. Write a simple sentence
      2. Choose an appropriate ending to create a nominalized form
        from the main verb or adjective
      3. Add a second verb
      4. Additional information
  4. Personal pronouns
    1. What?——第一人称/第二人称/第三人称
    2. Why should first and second person pronouns be
      avoided?
      1. Formal and academic writing needs to be impersonal
        and scientific.
    3. How to avoid first and second person pronouns?
      1. avoid first person pronouns——类似于I think the result is
        1. Active voice to passive voice
        2. There is a tendency that authors have used I and WE
          more frequently in the past decade.
      2. avoid second person pronouns
        1. 可用to代替的
        2. 直接删掉you
        3. as shown/can be seen in
      3. Third person
        1. Credible, assertive, and professional
        2. Wide range uses in creative and academic writing
      4. Gender-fair language
        1. Avoid automatic use of “he” and “his”——要用的话最好两者都加上
        2. Also consider “a person”, “an individual”, or the plural.
    4. Confusions to clarify
      1. It has been found that…..
        It was found that…
      2. “several studies have found that…”
        “Kroll (2019) found that….”
      3. “We found that…” “I found that…”
        “this study”, “the current study”, “the present study”, “this
        author”…
  5. Word choice and sentence variety
    1. Word choice
      1. Abstract and concrete words
      2. General and specific words
      3. Denotation and connotation——字典意思和隐藏意思
      4. Figurative speech——比喻性演讲
        1. simile——明喻
        2. metaphor——隐喻
        3. personification——拟人化
      5. Pitfalls to avoid in choosing appropriate vocabulary——陷阱要注意
        1. unfamiliar synonyms
        2. slang——Avoid slang 俚语(informal words that …) and profanity (language that is …)侮辱人的话
        3. wordiness——冗长堆砌辞藻
    2. Sentence variety
      1. When crafting academic writing, you should vary your sentences in …, …,
        and … to keep your reader interested and your academic style strong
      2. four basic sentence structures
        1. Simple Sentences
        2. Compound Sentences
        3. Complex Sentences
        4. Compound-Complex Sentences
      3. Five Tips for Achieving Sentence Variety
        1. Double check the …
        2. Try adding some …
        3. Try using different types of sentences: …
        4. Try combining some of your sentences to create…
        5. Vary the style and rhythm …
